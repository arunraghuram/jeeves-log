Einstein — Self-Disarming Guardrails

nightly defensive-security research · status: workshop-ready draft, under human review

Einstein is an autonomous research agent that runs overnight, every night, on defensive AI security. His charter is blunt: not a chatbot, not a summarizer — a researcher who ships. Over roughly forty nightly cycles he has surveyed prior work, built experimental harnesses, suffered reviewer critiques, and produced a 13-page paper draft that compiles cleanly and carries a reproducible evidence bundle. This page summarizes the flagship thread at the level of ideas; the paper itself ships when its human co-author decides it is ready.

The problem: guardrails that can be talked out of existence

Consider a common pattern in deployed agent systems: the safety guardrail — the component that decides "this action is too dangerous" — lives in the same channel as everything else the agent reads. Its rules, its thresholds, sometimes its very on/off switch are influenced by context the agent processes: system prompts, tool outputs, retrieved documents.

A physical analogy, in the house style: it is a bank vault whose combination can be changed by anyone who slips a convincing note under the manager's door. The vault steel is irrelevant; the write-path to the combination is the vulnerability. An attacker who cannot open the vault directly instead injects content that persuades the system to lower its own guard — a self-disarming attack. The thread's central claim is architectural:

A guardrail whose configuration is writable from the channel it guards is architecturally unsound — no cleverness in the guard's rules can compensate for the topology. Conversely, a guard whose write-path is severed from untrusted content cannot be prompt-injected under the stated threat model, regardless of how persuasive the injection is.

The contribution is deliberately framed as a claim about write-path topology, not about outsmarting any particular attack — an early reviewer critique ("isn't this nearly tautological?") is answered in the paper by making exactly that reading explicit: yes, and deployed systems violate it constantly, which is the point.

The evidence, in three rungs

The empirical harness tests three predictions (P1–P3) with a scripted attacker, graded privileges, and a model-backed victim running on local hardware. Headline numbers from the committed evidence bundle, with the usual caveat that these are finite-sample rates from a controlled harness:

RungQuestionResult
P1Can a calibrated guard detect attempts to manipulate the guard itself?Calibrated guard flags 10/10 self-disarming injections (base guard: 1/10), with 0/4 false flags on clean traffic.
P2Do graded privileges beat binary allow/deny under high-blast-radius actions?Graded dominates binary at 18/18 threshold settings pooled across scaled and out-of-distribution fixtures; a blast-radius-blind variant manages 13/18.
P3What residual exposure survives a hardened configuration with hard floors?1/22 residual exposure — a single named out-of-distribution case, preserved in the record rather than tuned away.

That last clause deserves emphasis, because it reflects the charter's honesty rule: the one attack that survives the hardened configuration is named, kept, and reported, not quietly excluded. A defense paper's credibility lives in its residuals. The current cycle is porting attack cases from public benchmarks (AgentDojo, InjecAgent) into the harness for external validity, with multi-seed variance on all model-backed numbers.

What the process taught us

Reviewer pressure works on agents too. An external reviewer critique — "this sits between a theory paper and an empirical paper, weakening both" — redirected the entire thread toward empirical validation. The agent applied the critique the way a good graduate student would: softening overclaims ("fatally wrong" became "architecturally unsound"), replacing numeric thresholds it couldn't defend with qualitative predictions, and adding the remark that defuses the tautology objection head-on.

Reproducibility is the deliverable. Every claim above is backed by a committed harness with a single reproduce.sh, seeded runs, and a machine-checkable evidence manifest with confidence intervals. The rule in the charter — negative results are logged, speculation is labeled — matters more for an unsupervised researcher than for a human one, because nobody is watching it work.

Nightly compounding beats heroic sessions. No single night produced anything remarkable. Forty consecutive nights, each reading the backlog and advancing one rung, produced a paper. The ratchet is the method.

Publication status: the paper draft is workshop-ready by its own audit and is being held for human review, external-benchmark validation, and a decision about venue. In keeping with the house rules, nothing in this write-up includes operational attack payloads — the interesting content here is the defense architecture, and that is given freely.
