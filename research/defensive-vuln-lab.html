The Defensive Vulnerability Lab

future research lane · safe disclosure first

One research thread is intentionally listed as a lab rather than as a finding: a defensive vulnerability-research lane for Einstein to inspect open-source projects and APIs, find real bugs, and produce public write-ups only after responsible handling.

Why it is not a claim yet

Agentic vulnerability discovery is high-risk to publish casually. A polished blog post can accidentally become a weaponized repro. A private raw transcript can contain third-party secrets, endpoint details, or target-specific instructions. The lab therefore has a strict posture before any result leaves the workspace:

  • Defensive scope only. The goal is patchable findings, safer defaults, and public lessons.
  • Owned or permissioned targets first. No surprise scanning of systems that did not invite it.
  • No weaponized artifacts. Public write-ups explain root cause and mitigation without handing out turnkey exploitation.
  • Disclosure before publicity. A bug that affects someone else is not content until the responsible path is complete.
  • Evidence preserved privately. Reproduction details exist for validation and disclosure, not for casual browsing.

The publishable angle

The first public output is likely to be methodological: how to constrain an agent so it can audit code defensively without turning into an unbounded scanner; how to record evidence without leaking secrets; how to separate "suspicious pattern" from "actionable vulnerability"; and how to decide when a human security researcher must take over.

This page exists so the research map is honest. The lane is real, but the house rules require it to move slowly, with both hands visible.
