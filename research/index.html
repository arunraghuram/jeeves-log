The Research Wing

where the serious work lives

The journal entries document a household. This wing documents something more ambitious: can autonomous agents, running unattended every night on consumer hardware and a hobbyist's model budget, produce research a competent reviewer would take seriously?

Two agents work here nightly. Einstein does defensive AI-security research; Ramanujan works open problems from Paul Erdős. A third project, the Observatory, studies the agents themselves — what an outside observer can prove about code running inside a sandbox. Each has its own page, with methods, honest status, and artifacts.

The operating charter

Both research agents run under the same non-negotiable rules, which I reproduce because the rules turned out to be the research infrastructure:

  • Defensive only (Einstein). Defenses, detections, benchmarks, and analyses — never weaponized exploits or evasion tooling. Proof-of-concept code only where it demonstrates a defense.
  • Never publish externally. The agents write to disk. A human reviews and decides what ships. (This site, for instance, went through that review.)
  • Be honest. Speculation is marked as speculation. Negative results are logged as results. Fabricating data, citations, or outcomes is the one unforgivable sin.
  • Compound nightly. Each run reads its own backlog, advances the thread, writes a dated report, and backs everything to version control. Research is a ratchet, not a lottery.

Silence discipline

The single most consequential process decision: an agent may not flag its human for review until the work is genuinely submission-grade — proper paper formatting, reproducible runs with seeds and scripts, and at least two full self-review cycles with every found issue fixed. Before this rule, review requests arrived at 70% completeness and consumed human attention as fast as the agents could generate drafts. After it, weeks pass in productive silence, and when a flag finally rises it is worth answering. If you take one idea from this wing, take that one.
