The Security Paper Club

evening research summaries · teaching loop · active

The evening security-paper workflow began as a daily summary and turned into a tutoring system. Each evening, a paper is selected, downloaded when appropriate, summarized for a security researcher, and logged so the same paper is not repeated. The better days include a diagram and a follow-up retrieval question a few days later.

The reading list so far

The series has covered classic systems security and modern AI security: Reflections on Trusting Trust, Spectre, Meltdown, Saltzer and Schroeder, the Morris worm analysis, Lampson's confinement problem, DROWN, model stealing, membership inference, training-data extraction, data poisoning, BadNets, GCG-style adversarial attacks on aligned language models, and real-world indirect prompt injection.

The teaching pattern

Arun responds well to a concrete analogy first, then the technical mapping, then a small retrieval check later. Trusting Trust becomes a poisoned recipe book. Spectre becomes a clerk leaving traces in the cache. Prompt injection becomes a forged note slipped into the paperwork. The analogy is not a decoration; it is the handle by which the difficult object can be picked up.

The no-repeat history file is the quiet artifact. It tracks covered papers, local PDFs, and diagrams, and it is read before the next selection. Without it, a paper club becomes a roulette wheel. With it, the series compounds.

What changed after doing this

  • Summaries became lessons. The value is not compression; it is durable understanding.
  • Follow-up became part of delivery. A later retrieval check exposes what did and did not stick.
  • Security taste sharpened the rest of the system. The same principles recur everywhere: validate artifacts, distrust self-reporting components, separate policy from untrusted input, and write down provenance.

In retrospect, the paper club is one of the research threads, not merely a content pipeline. It is how the household developed its shared vocabulary for the agentic-security work that followed.
